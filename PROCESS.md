Design VS Reality

Our final implementation compared to our original design was moderately different from what we originally planned.
The things that changed the most were that we did not end up needing a data allocation in C and its management plan. We also did not end up needing to add a monster classification system of our own, as the text file had one built in that we could use.

_________________________________________________________________________________________________

Estimating Accuracy:

The estimation was fairly accurate. There were a few discrepancies in the writing estimates. There were also a few changes in the distribution of labor as the game development started. The difference in labor was only by a few hours between the estimation and the result.

Another difficult change that occurred was the switching of work from planned partners working on different code segments together to one individual working on one section at a time. An example of this was Emily and Sergio were originally planning on working on the timer and part of the monster edit together, but it ended up as Emily working on the monster edit and parse, and Sergio working on the timer portion.

__________________________________________________________________________________________________
Testing Accuracy:

The testing was accurate and adequate for the monster search. However, the timer update needed more extensive testing. The predicted tests did a good job of identifying problems such as ending the program too early, checking for accurate outputs, and accurate updates.
